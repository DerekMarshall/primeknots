# Methods dossier — mechanism inventory

Evidence inventory for a methods paper on this project's human–AI collaboration protocol.
Every claim is cited to a commit hash, an errata row (`docs/ERRATA.md`), a ledger entry, a
pinning-note or PR locus, or a transcript locus. No result summary, no evaluation, no
recommendations. Sources of truth: `docs/ERRATA.md` (31 rows), `docs/preregistered/`
(PR-1..PR-3, LOOKS.md), `docs/notes/` (pinning logs, `reframe-registry.md`,
`referee-round-2026-07.md`, `layer1-qa-sweep.md`, `libm-partial-diff-spec.md`,
`eq1-interval.md`), the three claims ledgers (`docs/notes/data-note/CLAIMS-N.md`,
`docs/explainers/CLAIMS-E.md`, `docs/deck/CLAIMS.md`), and `docs/notes/data-note/README.md`
(house style). Commit citations are on `main` at HEAD `3a8916f`.

## 5. Known blind spots

Stated structurally; each cited to its instance. No internal mechanism of the type listed
detected these; the citation names the actual detector.

- **Shared-source transcription.** Two independent implementations that share one reading of a
  source cannot detect a mis-reading of that source; both agree on the wrong value. Instances:
  #25 (a constant typed from memory, shared by twins — detected by human read of the diff); #28
  (one reading of eq (2) carried into a pinning note, the C++ evaluator, and a PARI oracle —
  detected by the authors' published figure + an independent blind transcription of the source
  PDF, `docs/ERRATA.md` #25/#28, `docs/notes/referee-round-2026-07.md` Step 1). Structural
  boundary of twins (rule 2) and oracles (rule 3).
- **Approximation certificates that expire under formula correction.** A truncation/tolerance
  bound certified against a formula is invalid once the formula is corrected; the certificate
  does not self-invalidate. Instance: #29 — the weight-≥12 Hecke-truncation bound was certified
  against #28's corrupted products (under which no surviving term reached weight ≥12) and
  expired when #28 was corrected; detected by re-verification of every bound after the corrected
  reading (`docs/ERRATA.md` #29 and the sweep below it). The R1 density-evaluation tolerance
  (m4-pinning §R1, ≈0.056) folded in the same expired component.
- **Style regression in fresh prose.** A style checklist applied to existing prose does not
  prevent the same class of construct from entering newly written prose; it is caught only by a
  subsequent pass. Instance: the S7/S8 constructs (minted aphorism; "failed beneath", "point one
  way", "two faces") entered the reframe prose (commit `e0e52de`) and were removed only in the
  dedicated style pass (commit `18c1364`); S1–S6 were unnumbered prose in
  `data-note/README.md` until that pass formalized them as S1–S8.
